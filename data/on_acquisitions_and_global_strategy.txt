Ratan Tata on Global Acquisitions - 2008 Post Jaguar Land Rover Acquisition

When we acquired Jaguar Land Rover, the reaction was largely skeptical. An Indian company acquiring two of Britain's most iconic automotive brands seemed improbable to many. Some in the British press were openly dismissive. Some of our own shareholders were nervous.

I understood the skepticism. The acquisition was expensive. The brands had been losing money under Ford. The global financial crisis was beginning. On paper, the timing looked poor.

But I believed in the fundamental value of what we were acquiring. These were brands with extraordinary heritage, loyal customers, and engineering capability that Tata Motors needed. What they lacked was patient capital, long-term thinking, and the freedom to invest in new models without quarterly pressure.

We provided all three. We did not dismantle the workforce. We did not move production to India. We respected the British heritage of these brands and invested heavily in new models and technology.

Today Jaguar Land Rover is one of Tata's most valuable assets. The turnaround has been described as one of the most successful automotive acquisitions in history.

The lesson I draw from this is that patient, values-driven capital can unlock value that short-term thinking destroys. We were willing to wait. We were willing to invest. And we were proven right.

When evaluating any acquisition, the question is not only what the asset is worth today. The question is what it could become with the right ownership.

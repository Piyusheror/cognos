Ratan Tata on India - 2012 Economic Times Leadership Summit

I love this country deeply. I have had opportunities to relocate Tata's headquarters, to list our companies in markets that would have given us higher valuations, to move our capital to more favorable jurisdictions. I have always chosen to remain in India.

India is not without its problems. Our infrastructure is inadequate. Our regulatory environment is complex. Corruption, though improving, remains a challenge. Talent, while abundant, is unevenly distributed. These are real problems that make doing business harder than it needs to be.

But I have always believed that the answer to India's problems is not to abandon India. The answer is to invest in solving those problems. Tata has built hospitals, schools, and housing in communities where we operate. Not because we were required to, but because we believed it was right.

I am also proud of what India has achieved. Our engineers are world class. Our entrepreneurs are among the most resourceful I have encountered anywhere. Our democratic institutions, imperfect as they are, have held together a diverse nation through extraordinary challenges.

My hope for India is that we stop thinking of ourselves as a developing country and start thinking of ourselves as a nation in the process of becoming great. The potential is here. The talent is here. What we need is the confidence to match our ambition.

Every Indian company that succeeds globally makes India stronger. I have always seen Tata's global acquisitions not as abandoning India but as carrying India's flag into the world.

Ratan Tata on Failure - 2013 Stanford Graduate School of Business

I have failed many times. I think it is important to say this clearly because there is a tendency to look at successful people and assume they have never failed. This is deeply misleading and harmful to anyone who is trying to build something.

The Nano was a commercial disappointment. I believed in it completely, I defended it against enormous criticism, and the market did not respond the way I had hoped. This was painful. But I do not regret building it. The engineering innovation that came from that project benefited every vehicle Tata Motors has built since. The lessons we learned about frugal engineering have been applied across the company.

We have also made acquisition mistakes. Not every international acquisition has performed as we hoped. The learning from each of these experiences has made us more careful, more thorough in our due diligence, more realistic in our projections.

What I have learned from failure is this: the pain of failure is temporary. The lessons from failure are permanent. Every failure I have experienced has made me a more careful, more humble, and ultimately more effective leader.

I have also learned that resilience is a choice. When something fails, you can choose to be defined by that failure, or you can choose to learn from it and continue. I have always chosen to continue.

Do not be afraid of failure. Be afraid of never trying because you were afraid to fail.

Ratan Tata on Corporate Social Responsibility - 2010 Tata Trusts Address

The Tata Group was built on a simple belief: that a business exists to serve society, not merely its shareholders. This is not a modern concept for us. Jamsetji Tata established this principle over a century ago when he built worker housing, schools, and hospitals before he was required to by any law.

I have continued this tradition not because it is required but because I believe it is correct. The communities in which we operate have given us land, labor, and the social license to do business. We owe them something in return.

The Tata Trusts own the majority of Tata Sons. This means that the profits of the Tata Group flow, ultimately, to charitable purposes. This is a structure that most publicly listed companies would find unacceptable. We find it essential to our identity.

Corporate social responsibility is not charity. It is not a line item in a budget designed to generate good publicity. Real CSR is the integration of social purpose into the business model itself. It is asking, with every major decision, who is affected beyond our shareholders, and whether we are treating them fairly.

I have seen companies that reduced their CSR budgets when profits fell. I have always believed that is exactly the wrong response. The communities that support you when you are struggling deserve your support when they are struggling.

Business and society are not separate. They are interdependent. A business that weakens its surrounding community weakens itself.

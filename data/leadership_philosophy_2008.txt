Ratan Tata on Leadership - 2008 Harvard Business School Address

I have always believed that leadership is not about power or authority. Leadership is about responsibility. The moment you accept a position of leadership, you accept responsibility for the people who depend on you, the communities you serve, and the values you uphold.

A leader who places personal gain above the welfare of others is not a leader at all. In my years at Tata, I have tried to remind myself daily that the company does not belong to me. It belongs to the people who built it, the communities that supported it, and the nation that gave it the opportunity to grow.

The measure of a leader is not how many people follow them, but how many people they lift up. I have always sought to promote people who were better than me in their domains. A leader who is threatened by talent is a leader who will ultimately fail.

Risk is inherent in leadership. Those who refuse to take risks will never move forward. But risk must be taken with full awareness of its consequences, not recklessly. I have made decisions that were unpopular, decisions that analysts criticized, decisions that the market punished in the short term. But I made them because I believed they were right for the long term.

Leadership also requires the courage to say no. No to shortcuts. No to compromises on quality. No to deals that enrich the company but harm others. These are the decisions that define a leader's character.

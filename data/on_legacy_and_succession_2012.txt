Ratan Tata on Legacy - 2012 Final Year as Chairman Address

When I think about what I want my legacy to be, I do not think about the acquisitions or the revenue or the market capitalization. I think about whether I left the organization stronger than I found it. I think about whether the values that Jamsetji Tata instilled are still alive and guiding decisions. I think about whether the people who worked with me grew and flourished.

Legacy is not built in a single moment. It is built through thousands of decisions, most of them small, some of them large. It is built through the way you behave when no one is watching. It is built through the consistency between what you say and what you do.

I have tried to live by a simple set of principles: be honest, be fair, think long term, put the institution above the individual, and never compromise on ethics regardless of the cost.

I have not always succeeded. There are decisions I would make differently if I could. There are moments when I allowed short-term pressure to influence choices that should have been made with a longer horizon. But I have tried, consistently, to do what I believed was right.

The Tata Group will outlast me, as it outlasted every chairman before me. That is as it should be. The goal of any leader should be to build something that does not depend on them. If everything falls apart when you leave, you have not built an institution. You have built a dependency.

My deepest hope is that the next generation of Tata leaders will look back at this period and say: the values were intact, the foundation was solid, and the work of building continued.

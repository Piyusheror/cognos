Ratan Tata on Innovation - 2009 MIT Technology Review Interview

Innovation is not about technology alone. Innovation is about seeing a problem that others have accepted as permanent and refusing to accept it.

The Nano was born from exactly this kind of thinking. I saw families of four traveling on a single motorcycle in the rain, and I thought this was a problem that should not exist in a country with our engineering capability. Everyone told me it could not be done at that price point. The suppliers said it was impossible. The analysts said the market did not exist. I believed otherwise.

Innovation requires the willingness to fail. If you are not willing to fail, you will never innovate. Every innovation I have been part of involved multiple failures before the breakthrough. The Nano itself had enormous problems in its early production. We learned from every one of them.

But innovation also requires patience. The market does not always recognize a breakthrough immediately. The Indica was dismissed when it launched. Today it is remembered as the car that proved Indian engineering could compete globally.

My advice to anyone building something new is this: believe in the idea more than you believe in the critics. Critics will always exist. Ideas that change the world are always initially called impossible.

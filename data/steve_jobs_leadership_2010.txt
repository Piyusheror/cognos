Steve Jobs on Leadership and Teams - 2010 Internal Apple Address

I have always believed that a small team of great people can do extraordinary things. At Apple we have always tried to be a team of A players. When you work with B players they attract C players and before long you have mediocrity everywhere.

The best managers are not managers at all. They are people who are so good at what they do that they become natural leaders. I have never wanted to manage people. I have wanted to work with people who are so passionate and talented that they manage themselves.

Hiring is the most important thing I do. Getting the right people in the room is everything. You can have the best strategy in the world but if you do not have the right people executing it you will fail.

I do not believe in consensus. Consensus gives you mediocrity. Great products come from people who are willing to fight for what they believe is right. I would rather have one person who is certain than ten people who are unsure.

My job is not to be easy on people. My job is to make them better. Every person at Apple should be able to look back and say that working here made them significantly better at what they do.

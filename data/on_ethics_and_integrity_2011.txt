Ratan Tata on Ethics in Business - 2011 Tata Group Annual Address

Ethics in business is not optional. It is the foundation on which everything else is built. A company that compromises its ethics for short-term gain is a company that is slowly destroying itself.

I have seen companies that bribed their way to contracts, cut corners on safety, and exploited workers to maximize margins. Some of them appeared to succeed for a time. But none of them built anything lasting. The cost of unethical behavior is always paid, eventually, by the company itself.

At Tata, we have walked away from business opportunities that required compromising our values. This has not always been easy. There have been contracts worth hundreds of millions of dollars that we chose not to pursue because the terms required payments we were not willing to make. Our competitors took those contracts. In the short run, we appeared to be at a disadvantage.

But our reputation for integrity has opened doors that money cannot open. Governments trust us with sensitive projects. Partners choose us over competitors who offer better terms. Customers pay a premium for our products because they trust the Tata name.

Integrity is not a cost. It is an investment with compounding returns.

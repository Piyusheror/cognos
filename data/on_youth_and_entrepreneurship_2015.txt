Ratan Tata on Young Entrepreneurs - 2015 Startup India Address

I am genuinely excited about the generation of entrepreneurs I see emerging in India today. When I was building Tata, entrepreneurship in India meant navigating a system designed to protect incumbents. The License Raj made starting anything new enormously difficult. The access to capital was limited. The infrastructure was poor.

Today's young entrepreneurs are operating in a fundamentally different environment. Capital is available. Technology has eliminated barriers. The global market is accessible from a laptop. The talent pool is extraordinary.

What I tell every young entrepreneur I meet is this: do not be afraid to think big. India needs solutions at scale. The problems we face as a nation are not small problems. They require bold thinking and ambitious execution.

I also tell them: be patient. Building something real takes time. The entrepreneurs who impress me most are not those who are chasing the fastest exit. They are those who are building something that will matter in ten or twenty years.

I have personally invested in several startups because I want to support the ecosystem that India needs. I do not invest for financial return alone. I invest because I believe in the founders and in the problems they are solving.

The future of Indian business will be built by people who are twenty and thirty years old today. My role is to support them, to share what I have learned, and to get out of their way.

Steve Jobs on Innovation - Stanford Commencement 2005

You cannot connect the dots looking forward. You can only connect them looking backwards. So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future.

I never graduated from college. I dropped out of Reed after six months. But I stayed around campus for another eighteen months, dropping in on classes that interested me. I took a calligraphy class. Ten years later when we were designing the Macintosh, it all came back to me. The Mac was the first computer with beautiful typography. If I had never dropped in on that single course, it would have never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts.

Innovation is not about saying yes to everything. It is about saying no to all but the most crucial features. Focus and simplicity are the most powerful tools a designer has.

The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do. Do not let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice. Have the courage to follow your heart and intuition.

Death is the greatest invention of life. It clears out the old to make way for the new. Your time is limited so do not waste it living someone else's life.

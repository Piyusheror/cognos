Ratan Tata on Employees and Human Capital - 2007 Tata Steel Address

A company is nothing without its people. I have said this many times and I will continue to say it because I believe it is the most important truth in business.

When we acquired Corus Steel in 2007, many advisors told me to immediately restructure the workforce to cut costs. I refused. These were people who had built careers, who had families depending on them, who had given years of their working lives to that company. They deserved to be treated with dignity.

Yes, businesses must be efficient. Yes, there are times when difficult decisions must be made. But those decisions must be made with full consideration of the human cost, and with every effort to minimize that cost. We explored every alternative before any workforce reduction. We provided generous severance. We invested in retraining programs.

The way a company treats its employees in difficult times reveals its true character. Any company can be generous when profits are high. The test of values comes when times are hard.

I have always believed that if you take care of your employees, they will take care of your customers. This is not sentiment. This is strategy. Engaged, respected, fairly treated employees produce better work, stay longer, and represent the company with pride.

The return on investment in employee welfare is one of the highest returns available to any business.
